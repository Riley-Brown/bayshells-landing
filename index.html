Cleaning

    Daily:
  • Wipe down your boat with a clean dry towel after every row
  • If you regularly row in salt or brackish water, it is recommended to wash your boat with a non-abrasive cleaner or soap and ronse woth fresh water.
  • Wipe down seat tracks and oar locks with a clean, damp towel and make sure they stay clear of dirt, sand and other particles.
  • Open hatches and plugs so that the airtight hull can breathe.
    Periodically:
  • Make sure all the nuts and bolts are attached and tight.
  • Spray racing sandals with an anti-fungal spray.
  • Cleaning your boat with a non-abrasive cleaner will help mantain the look of your hull. Never use any products containing bleach on your boat.
    Yearly:

HISTORY

Bay Shells Rowing has not always been recognized by the name and boat it sells today. It has a colorful history that began with open water rowing legend Gordon Nash. In 1978, he developed three boats: the Pacific 18, Pacific 24 and the Pacific 30. He began manufacturing these boats at Pacific Rowing Crafters in Sausalito, CA. The Pacific 24 quickly gained a reputaion as a fast open water boat having been designed so that the center of buoyancy is forward of the cockpit, whick causes the bow to rise in rough water. The Pacific 18 was a popular entry level boat. The pacific 24 long held records in the Isles of the Shoals, Catalina, Monterrey and Santa Cruz races. In 1987, Nash sold this “plant” to an investor group called Rowtech.
